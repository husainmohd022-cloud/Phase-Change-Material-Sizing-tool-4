⚑ Special Parameters & Flags — Most Commonly Misunderstood
f_usable
Usable Latent Fraction (default: 0.9) In real systems, PCM never melts 100% uniformly. Poor thermal contact, voids in the material, and uneven heat distribution mean only a fraction of the latent heat is actually accessible. A value of 0.9 means 90% of the rated latent heat capacity is counted as usable. Reduce this if your PCM has poor thermal conductivity or uncertain contact quality with the cell surface.
SF
Safety Factor (default: 1.2) A design margin applied to the final PCM mass result to account for real-world uncertainties — manufacturing tolerances, cell aging, ambient temperature variation, and property data scatter. SF = 1.2 sizes the PCM 20% above the theoretical minimum. In worst-case mode, SF is automatically enforced to a minimum of 1.5.
DCIR
DC Internal Resistance The resistance of the cell measured under direct current conditions — not AC impedance. This governs how much electrical energy is lost as heat during charge or discharge. DCIR increases with cell age, low state of charge, and low temperature, making it a key driver of thermal load.
C-rate
Charge / Discharge Rate Convention Defines the speed of charge or discharge relative to the cell's capacity. A 1C rate means full discharge in one hour. In this tool, C-rate also determines the thermal exposure duration — higher C-rates mean longer total heat generation windows, which directly drives the PCM sizing.
